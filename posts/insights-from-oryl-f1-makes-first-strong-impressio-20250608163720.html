Understanding the Problem: Solubility and Aggregation in Drug Development

Solubility and aggregation are critical factors in drug development, as they can significantly impact a molecule's efficacy and stability. Currently, measuring these properties requires manual labor-intensive processes, which can be time-consuming and costly. The lack of standardized methods for solubility and aggregation measurement has led to a significant gap in the market, making it challenging for researchers to make informed decisions.

The ORYL F1: A Game-Changer in Solubility and Aggregation Measurement

The ORYL F1 is designed to bridge this gap by providing a high-throughput, automated platform for measuring solubility and aggregation of drug molecules. This instrument utilizes second harmonic scattering, a laser-technology known for its unparalleled sensitivity in probing the structure of materials at molecular level.

Key Features and Advantages

The ORYL F1 offers several key features that set it apart from existing methods:

  • High-throughput capabilities: The instrument can measure 384 well-plates in just 15 minutes, significantly increasing throughput and unlocking solubility and aggregation measurement at scale.
  • Automated format: The ORYL F1 is designed to be user-friendly, with an automated format adapted to 96 and 384 well-plates.
  • Cost savings: The instrument achieves significant cost savings by simplifying the measurement process while preserving samples for orthogonal measurements and kinetics studies.
