Cute, but powerful: meet NanoCluster, a tiny supercomputer

In the world of computing, it's not often that you come across a device that's both adorable and packed with power. But that's exactly what Cyped has created with their NanoCluster, a tiny supercomputer that's smaller than a coffee mug. With the ability to hold up to seven Raspberry Pi compute modules, this little device is promising to deliver impressive performance and flexibility.

What is NanoCluster?

NanoCluster is a prototype of Cypedes nano cluster, which promises to be a small, powerful computer cluster. It's designed to work with various compute modules, including the popular Raspberry Pi Compute Modules. The device itself is small enough to fit in a coffee mug, making it an attractive option for anyone looking for a compact computing solution.

Key Features

So, what makes NanoCluster tick? Here are some of its key features:

  • Seven-node cluster: NanoCluster can hold up to seven Raspberry Pi compute modules, making it a powerful computing solution.
  • M.2 slots: The device uses M.2 slots, which require adapter boards to connect the compute modules.
  • PoE power: NanoCluster supports PoE (Power over Ethernet) power, making it easy to set up and use.
  • Managed network switch: The device includes a managed network switch that allows for VLAN setup and port management.
  • BMC: There's also a lightweight BMC (Baseboard Management Controller) that enables remote management of the device.

How does NanoCluster work?

While we can't get into too much detail without actually testing it, we can say that NanoCluster is designed to be modular. The compute modules are connected using adapter boards, and each module has its own UART port for serial console access. This makes it easy to set up and manage the device.

What's next?

NanoCluster is currently on pre-sale, with prices starting at around $40 for the base model. While we can't wait to get our hands on one, there are some caveats to consider. The device requires a few adapter boards, and some compute modules may have compatibility issues.

Impact/Significance

So, why should you care about NanoCluster? Here are a few reasons:

  • Compact computing: NanoCluster offers a compact computing solution that's perfect for small spaces.
  • Powerful performance: With up to seven Raspberry Pi Compute Modules, this device is capable of delivering impressive performance.
  • Flexibility: The modular design makes it easy to customize and upgrade the device.

Conclusion

NanoCluster is an innovative device that promises to deliver powerful computing performance in a compact package. While there are some caveats to consider, we're excited to see what this tiny supercomputer can do. Stay tuned for more updates on NanoCluster!
